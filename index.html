About Me

I am an AI-focused researcher with deep interests in Large Language Models, deep learning, and intelligent systems. Proficient in C, C++, Java, and Python, I build and study AI systems from the ground up — from neural network architectures to LLM inference pipelines.

My current research at USTC centers on optimizing cloud-based LLM deployment — exploring KV cache management, continuous batching, speculative decoding, and distributed scheduling to make AI inference faster and more efficient at scale.

Beyond academics, I served as Regional Coordinator of the Association of Timorese Students in China, and as a Youth Facilitator at Plan International — reflecting my commitment to community and leadership.
